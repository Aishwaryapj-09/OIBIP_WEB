Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, into a Punjabi family. From the age of three, he showed a natural love for cricket, often playing with a bat at home. He studied at Vishal Bharti Public School before joining the West Delhi Cricket Academy in 1998. Under coach Rajkumar Sharma, Virat developed discipline and skill that set him apart from others. He later moved to Saviour Convent School to balance studies with cricket training. Known for his energy and focus, he rarely missed practice sessions as a child.

Virat Kohli was born into a close-knit Punjabi family in Delhi. His father, Prem Kohli, was a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a homemaker. He grew up with two siblings — an elder brother, Vikas Kohli, and an elder sister, Bhavna Kohli. His father supported his cricketing dream until his untimely death in 2006. In his personal life, Virat found love in Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma. After years of being one of India’s most admired celebrity couples, they tied the knot on December 11, 2017.

Domestic

  • Joined West Delhi Cricket Academy in 1998 and excelled in junior cricket for Delhi.
  • Top run-scorer for Delhi U-15 and U-17, winning the Vijay Merchant Trophy in 2003–04.
  • Led India U-19 to victory in the 2008 World Cup in Malaysia.

International Career

  • ODI debut for India against Sri Lanka in August 2008, establishing himself as a reliable top-order batsman.
  • Scored his first ODI century in 2009 against Sri Lanka, showcasing his ability in chase
  • Took over India’s Test captaincy in 2014 and became all-format captain in 2017.

captaincy Journey

  • Took over as Test captain in 2014 after MS Dhoni’s retirement.
  • Led India to its first-ever Test series win in Australia (2018–19).
  • Made India the No. 1 ranked Test team for several years.

IPL CAREER

  • Picked by Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) in 2008 and has been loyal since.
  • Became RCB captain in 2013, leading until 2021
  • Had a historic 2016 IPL season with 973 runs and 4 centuries.
